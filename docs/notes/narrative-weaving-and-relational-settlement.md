# Narrative Weaving and Relational Settlement

## Core claim

The narrator is not merely a late linguistic gloss laid over already-finished experience. Nor is experience best modeled as a single path through an undifferentiated latent space. What is more plausible is a **narrator-weaver**: a process that settles candidate short trajectories — **moment arcs** — into a coherent self-body-world unfolding. Felt reality is not the output of that settlement. **Felt reality is the enacted settlement process itself.**

This note sharpens three points:

1. the move from point-state to **moment-arc assembly**,
2. the move from a single path to a **relationally structured evolving assembly**, and
3. the role of **linguistic narrative** as a lagged but active coherence constraint, not a passive replay.

---

## 1. From narrator to narrator-weaver

The older “narrator” framing usefully highlighted that experience is not a direct readout of the world. But it risked sounding too much like there are already-finished events and then a later voice that merely describes or rationalizes them.

That is too weak.

A better framing is that there is an ongoing **weaving** or **settlement** process that:

- proposes candidate short arcs rather than isolated instantaneous states,
- evaluates them under local and global coherence constraints,
- keeps multiple candidates provisionally available,
- allows limited retroactive revision,
- and only gradually stabilizes what “was happening.”

This preserves what was good about the narrator idea — the importance of integration, model-building, and retrospective pressure — without reducing it to linguistic commentary.

The core change is:

> The unit of commitment is not the frame but the **arc**.

The phenomenal now is better understood as a **softly committed short trajectory segment** rather than a durationless point.

---

## 2. Moment-arc assembly

Instead of the system choosing a best next state, it may be choosing among candidate **short path segments**.

Each candidate arc is evaluated by some mixture of:

- local smoothness,
- fit to incoming signals,
- compatibility with nearby candidate arcs,
- admissibility under the longer-term self/world geometry,
- and downstream usefulness for maintaining a coherent trajectory.

Commitment is not binary. Earlier arcs can be revised, but revision becomes more expensive the more those arcs have become entangled with:

- later arcs,
- memory,
- action,
- self-description,
- and other stabilized commitments.

So “what happened” is not fixed all at once. It is gradually settled.

This yields a more mechanistic version of a multiple-drafts picture:

- candidate arcs coexist,
- later evidence can retroactively shift plausibility,
- commitment is graded,
- and the apparent directness of experience is the mark of sufficiently settled equilibrium.

---

## 3. Beyond a single path: structured evolving assemblies

The phrase “movie in latent space” helps, but it can also obscure something important. A single path through a latent space makes continuity visible, but it hides:

- recursive structure,
- typed relations,
- nested self-modeling,
- grammatical and role-like organization,
- paths about paths,
- and higher-order coalitions.

Human experience is not just a smooth curve. It is a **structured evolving object**.

So the right mathematical intuition is not a single point moving through a space, but something more like:

- a set of modality- or role-specific projections,
- plus higher-order relations among them,
- plus short trajectory segments through time,
- all jointly settled.

That means a moment is not an isolated point. It is an **organized state-complex**.

---

## 4. Local manifolds and joint settlement

A useful low-dimensional picture is this.

Imagine a shared 3D ambient space used purely for intuition. Embedded in it are several 2D manifolds, each representing the allowable projections for a modality or typed role:

- a visual manifold,
- a vestibular manifold,
- a proprioceptive manifold,
- a body-model manifold,
- perhaps also self-location, affect, action-readiness, etc.

The important point is that a projected point on one manifold is **not** a primitive atom or objective given. It is itself a candidate projection onto an allowable local manifold. And that projection is not settled in isolation.

At the same time, candidate points across manifolds are linked into candidate figures:

- lines,
- polygons,
- polyhedra,
- or more abstract higher-order relational structures.

The candidate points and candidate figures are **jointly settled**.

So it is not:

1. determine the points,
2. then connect them.

It is:

1. propose candidate local projections and candidate higher-order relations,
2. let them constrain each other,
3. settle together into a lower-energy structured assembly.

This is one reason the graph / hypergraph / simplicial-complex language is useful.

- A **graph** gives pairwise relations.
- A **hypergraph** gives multi-way coalitions.
- A **simplicial complex** gives higher-order coalitions together with inherited lower-order subrelations.

The last of these is especially suggestive because it captures the idea that when a higher-order coalition stabilizes, many lower-order relations come with it automatically.

Still, all of these are only partial pictures. The “nodes” are not fixed discrete atoms. They are themselves provisional local projections within a larger settlement process.

So the better statement is:

> The nodes are themselves products of the same settlement process that organizes them.

---

## 5. Two levels of geometry, or better, two levels of energy

The shared ambient-space picture is useful because it reveals two distinct levels of constraint.

### 5.1 Local manifold geometry

Each manifold has an intrinsic geometry or local energy landscape. This determines how costly it is to move within that modality/role’s allowable space.

This encodes things like:

- which local transformations are cheap,
- which directions are stiff,
- which projections are naturally nearby,
- and how the manifold’s learned curvature shapes local admissibility.

### 5.2 Assembly-level geometry / energy

Even if each local projection is cheap on its own manifold, the **joint configuration** may still be expensive.

This is where the ambient-space intuition helps. It makes clear that there can be a distinct notion of cost in the cross-manifold linkages:

- tension,
- mismatch,
- span,
- incompatibility,
- or deformation of the overall assembly.

So there is a difference between:

- what each modality can cheaply become,
- and what the system can cheaply become **jointly**.

This suggests that “metric tensor” is useful up to a point, but that the more general and probably better intuition is an **energy functional**.

## 6. Spring visualization

A simple visualization is to imagine two wavy surfaces, one floating above the other, roughly parallel but with varying distance between them due to their local curvature. Pick one point on each surface and connect them by a little spring.

Now imagine each point moving along its own surface through time.

What happens?

A point on one surface may have a cheaper route according to its own surface alone, but because it is coupled by the spring to the point on the other surface, the cheapest **joint** path may force it to “take the long way.”

This picture makes several things vivid:

- local cheapness and joint cheapness can come apart,
- a globally better assembly can require locally worse moves,
- the relevant object is not just two points but a paired trajectory,
- and the energy lives over a short path segment, not just at an instant.

The springs are only a simple case. More generally, the coupling energy could depend on:

- type labels,
- direction,
- relative orientation,
- coalition membership,
- temporal alignment,
- or higher-order multi-way compatibility.

The point is not literal springs. The point is that **relational constraints can alter which local paths are globally cheapest**.

## 7. Ketamine and incompatibility settlement

This picture becomes especially useful when thinking about ketamine phenomenology.

One plausible speculation is that ketamine disrupts something like **coincidence determination** or micro-temporal binding. Signals that would normally be assigned to slightly different times may be provisionally treated as co-members of the same moment arc.

Suppose a vestibular projection and a visual projection are provisionally grouped together in a way that creates a highly strained assembly. The system does not necessarily experience this as naked incompatibility or white noise.

Instead, if the settlement process is trying to find a lower-energy short trajectory that lets these projections belong together, it may settle on a path in which:

- the body is twisting,
- being squeezed around a bend,
- being accelerated,
- or being torqued.

That path is not a mere explanation layered on afterward. The settled body-trajectory **is the felt event**.

So the stronger speculative claim is:

> When temporally mismatched projections are provisionally treated as co-members of the same moment, the system does not experience their incompatibility as such. It experiences the low-energy bodily trajectory that best reconciles them.

That seems to capture why ketamine distortions are often:

- smooth rather than noisy,
- bodily rather than abstractly contradictory,
- and compelling rather than obviously confabulatory.

The experience is the settlement.

---

## 8. Why the path is not enough

All of the above richer machinery can, in principle, be represented as a single point moving through a sufficiently high-dimensional configuration space.

That is mathematically natural and often useful. It is like representing three particles in 3D as one point in a much larger configuration space.

So the richer picture does **not** rule out a point-trajectory representation.

But it is conceptually worse for present purposes, because it hides the factorization that matters:

- local modality/role manifolds,
- higher-order relational settlement,
- temporal arc commitment,
- lagged revision,
- and typed narrative structure.

So the point-trajectory view is a legitimate flattening, but a flattening nonetheless.

---

## 9. The central point about narrative

There is a temptation, once the geometric and energetic machinery is in place, to think that linguistic narrative has been reduced to a late add-on.

That would be a mistake.

The linguistic layer is not the source of felt reality, but it is also not a passive replay. Because commitment is graded and settlement remains provisional for some span, the eventual **linguistic summarization** can exert retroactive force on the trajectory.

Why? Because once a later verbal summary exists, it becomes another coherence constraint in the larger settlement process.

The system now has pressure not only for:

- moment arcs to fit together,
- bodily and sensory projections to fit together,
- memory traces to fit later action and interpretation,

but also for:

- the trajectory to fit the story eventually told about it.

So linguistic narrative can push earlier weakly committed arcs toward alignment with later explicit tellability.

This does **not** mean language creates experience from nowhere. It means language is a **lagged but dynamically active coherence field** over the settlement of experience.

This preserves what was valuable in the original narrator idea.

Language is special because it introduces:

- explicit recursion,
- reflexive self-modeling,
- grammatical nesting,
- social legibility,
- durable summarization,
- and a manipulable metamodel of what happened.

These do not merely restate the trajectory. They can participate in its eventual stabilization.

So the mature view is:

- the deeper process is not merely linguistic,
- but neither is language merely decorative.

It is one of the forces that can help determine what the low-energy trajectory is taken to have been.

That is why therapy, journaling, confession, explanation, and retrospective verbal framing can feel so powerful. They are not always just changing beliefs about fixed past experiences. They can participate in the still-ongoing stabilization of the trajectory itself.

This is the central point:

> Linguistic narrative is not just a gloss on settled experience. It is a higher-order, lagged, recursively structured constraint that can feed back into the settlement of moment arcs and help determine which trajectory stabilizes as having happened.

---

## 10. Provisional synthesis

A moment is not a point-state. It is a **softly settled short trajectory of a relationally structured self-body-world assembly**.

The local projections that participate in this assembly are themselves provisional and constrained by modality- or role-specific allowable manifolds.

The joint assembly is shaped by a composite energy functional that includes:

- local manifold costs,
- cross-manifold interaction costs,
- higher-order coalition costs,
- temporal smoothness costs,
- revision / commitment costs,
- and, for human beings, lagged narrative coherence costs.

The resulting experience is not the output of this process. It is the **structural enactment of the settlement process itself**.

Or more simply:

> Felt reality is the enacted settling of candidate self-body-world trajectories under structured local, relational, temporal, and narrative constraints.

That appears to preserve the best of both pictures:

- the geometric and energetic richness needed for smoothness and bodily coherence,
- and the recursive, reflexive, grammatical richness needed to explain why narrative matters.
