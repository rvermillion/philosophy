# Uptakeities

*A coinage, on the model of Dennett’s “deepity.” Where a deepity is a statement that looks profound by trading on an ambiguity between trivial and false readings, an uptakeity is something that looks like it’s hiding a similar trick — “how can something real depend on being believed in?” — but where the apparent circularity turns out to be load-bearing architecture rather than rhetorical sleight-of-hand. Uptake-dependence, in the uptakeity case, isn’t a defect. It’s the mechanism.*

## Core concept

An **uptakeity** is a thing whose existence, persistence, status, or functioning depends on being taken up — believed in, committed to, recognized, treated as real. The category includes beliefs, desires, selves, categories, games, truths-within-games, institutions, contracts, money, authority, legitimacy, expertise, relationships, promises, scientific paradigms, ongoing traditions.

Uptakeities are fully real in the operative sense: they constrain action, organize perception, and produce consequences. But their reality is of a specific kind — it’s sustained by practices of uptake rather than by uptake-independent facts. This is not a mark against them. It’s a different mode of being real than the mode planets and electrons have.

The critic’s move — “if something depends on being believed in, it’s not really real” — relies on a single-standard-of-reality assumption that the uptakeity concept flatly denies. There are uptake-independent things and there are uptakeities. Both can be fully real within their respective modes. Conflating “real” with “uptake-independent” is itself the error.

## The diagnostic distinction from deepities

A genuine deepity collapses under pressure: ask “which reading do you mean?” and the apparent profundity evaporates into either triviality or falsehood. A genuine uptakeity doesn’t collapse — pressing on it reveals a real dependency structure between the thing and the practices that sustain it. The pressure-test distinguishes them: deepities dissolve, uptakeities reveal.

This gives the term operationalizable content. An uptakeity isn’t just something uptake-dependent; it’s something whose uptake-dependence, examined carefully, turns out to be the key to understanding how the thing works rather than a reason to dismiss it.

## Two cross-cutting axes

Uptakeities sort along at least two independent dimensions.

### Axis 1: Temporal-causal relation of uptake to existence

- **Sustaining**: uptake maintains something that already exists. Most conventions, ongoing institutions, stable social roles. Money in a stable economy.
- **Constitutive**: uptake creates the thing at the moment of uptake. Promises, contracts, performative speech acts.
- **Hyperstitional**: uptake anticipates and pulls into being something whose existence requires the anticipatory belief as causal factor. Self-fulfilling prophecies, bootstrapped movements, paradigms that summon their phenomena. (Hyperstition in the CCRU sense is this subclass specifically — not all uptakeities, just this one.)
- **Degenerative**: uptake sustains something that’s losing material support, holding it in existence past the point where its non-uptake substrates would carry it. Dying traditions kept alive by committed believers. Hyperstitional-in-reverse.

### Axis 2: Role of uptake in the thing’s functioning

- **Recognitionsl uptakeities**: uptake sustains existence but isn’t part of the mechanism by which the thing does its work. Mostly formal or nominal cases.
- **Functional uptakeities**: *uptake, binding, commitment, belief is required for the thing to function as the kind of thing it is.*

Functional uptake-dependence is the sharper, more architecturally interesting case. Uptake isn’t external scaffolding holding up an otherwise-inert thing — uptake is a working part. Remove the uptake and the thing doesn’t just lose its existence; it loses its capacity to do what it does, because doing what it does was partly constituted by being taken up.

## The money test

Money illustrates functional uptakeity cleanly. A dollar isn’t functioning as money merely because people *recognize* it as historically-designated currency. It functions as money because it’s being *taken up* as spendable, price-bearing, obligation-settling. If everyone still recognized the bills historically but no longer treated them as spendable, they would no longer function as money in the relevant sense. Confederate dollars in a museum are the limit case: full historical recognition, zero functional uptake, no current monetary capacity.

Recognition without functional uptake produces relics. Functional uptake is what keeps the thing live.

This holds even more strongly for beliefs, desires, selves, categories, and games:

- A belief you recognize historically (“I used to believe that”) but no longer take up as true is no longer functioning as a belief — it’s a remembered former belief, which is a different thing.
- A category you recognize but no longer use to sort cases is no longer functioning as a category.
- A game you know the rules of but aren’t playing isn’t functioning as a game.
- A self that’s recognized (by others, by memory) but no longer narrated in the first-person present tense isn’t functioning as a self in the load-bearing sense — it’s a biographical subject, not a live agent.

In each case, the thing is not merely uptake-sustained. Its capacity to do its work is thresholded on the commitment-strength of the uptake.

## Why functional uptakeities require binding-for-now

This is the cross-connection to the binding-under-constraint note. Functional uptakeities are exactly the class of things that require the binding-for-now engagement mode.

Non-functional uptakeities can be held weakly without losing them — you can acknowledge that “Tuesday” is a convention without Tuesday ceasing to be Tuesday for you. Functional uptakeities can’t: if you hold your beliefs weakly enough to foreground their uptake-dependence in real time, they stop functioning as beliefs. The thing’s functional capacity is degraded by explicit attention to its uptake-dependent character.

The sharper statement:

> **Functional uptakeities require binding-for-now as their engagement mode, because holding them with full awareness of their uptake-dependent character would impair the very function the uptake constitutes.**

The narrator’s job, when handling functional uptakeities, is to locally foreground the thing’s reality and locally background its uptake-dependence — not because the uptake-dependence is false but because explicit attention to it would degrade the function. The witness, running in parallel, tracks the uptake-dependence so the narrator doesn’t have to. The division of labor from the binding note has its specific rationale here: functional uptakeities are precisely the class that requires it.

## Sharper process-priority diagnostic

The generic process-priority diagnostic asks: *what process had to run for this apparent primitive to become available as an object at all?*

The uptakeity-specific refinement, applied to functional uptakeities: *what process of ongoing uptake does this thing’s continued functioning depend on, and what would change about its functional capacity if that uptake were withdrawn or weakened?*

This is more operationalizable than the generic version. It points to specific things to investigate — the ongoing commitments, the coordination mechanisms, the failure modes under reduced uptake — rather than just the general suggestion that a process must be somewhere in the background.

## Cross-axis taxonomy: where the interesting cases sit

The two axes cross-cut. Most of the things the broader framework has been working on are in the **functional** column across multiple temporal-causal rows:

- **Functional-sustaining**: beliefs, ongoing categories, money, established institutions, long-running games. Live by continuous uptake.
- **Functional-constitutive**: promises, performative declarations, moments of commitment. Live by the uptake-act itself.
- **Functional-hyperstitional**: emerging movements, aspirational identities, paradigms in formation, bootstrapping projects. Live by anticipatory uptake pulling reality forward.
- **Functional-degenerative**: dying institutions, decaying authorities, traditions on life support. Live past their substrate by uptake alone.

What all four share is the functional column — which is why binding-for-now is the common engagement mode regardless of the temporal-causal placement.

## Framework integration

Uptakeity may be the genus; several existing projects are species-level studies of specific uptakeity phenomena:

- **Liminalism** studies boundary-maintenance of uptakeities — what makes them hold vs. fragment.
- **Taxonomy-as-game** studies uptakeities at the category level.
- **Games of Parts** studies the self as a functional uptakeity composed of sub-uptakeities.
- **Metadialectics** studies conflicts between uptakeities at the game-level.
- **Binding-under-constraint** studies the engagement mode that functional uptakeities require.
- **Process-priority diagnostic** (generalized) asks about the uptake-history of apparent primitives.

If this framing holds up, the various frameworks aren’t loosely connected — they’re studies of different aspects of a single underlying phenomenon. The self is a functional uptakeity that happens to constitute the agent. Other functional uptakeities constitute other structures the agent engages with.

## Honest vs. dishonest hyperstitions

Since hyperstition is one subclass, a note on its ethical structure: the concept gives us language to distinguish between honest hyperstitions (openly attempting to summon something, explicitly anticipatory, inviting others to join the summoning with clear eyes) and dishonest ones (concealing their hyperstitional structure, claiming to describe rather than summon, presenting anticipatory belief as if it were recognition of pre-existing fact).

Guardian Angel, Visible Seams, academic research programs, startup narratives, movement-building — all potentially honest hyperstitions, because the anticipatory nature can be made legible to participants. Ponzi schemes, astroturfed movements, certain kinds of political myth-making — dishonest hyperstitions, because they rely on participants not noticing the anticipatory structure.

The honest/dishonest distinction is orthogonal to the functional/non-functional axis — both honest and dishonest hyperstitions can be functional. What makes a hyperstition ethical isn’t that it’s hyperstitional but what it’s summoning and whether the summoning is transparent about what it’s doing.

## Status

New thread, generalizes and unifies several existing ones. The functional uptakeity concept in particular does substantial work — it’s probably the most important single term to preserve, because it’s what the binding note, the narrator-weaver note, and the Arrow note have all been implicitly about.

Possible extensions:

- The anti-reflexive subclass of functional uptakeities (things that lose function if the uptaker explicitly knows they’re performing uptake — certain authorities, certain kinds of enchantment, possibly some aesthetic experiences)
- The stability/fragility of different uptakeities under philosophical attention — which survive explicit analysis and which degrade
- Whether all institutional facts (Searle) are uptakeities or only a subset
- The relationship between functional uptakeity and Hacking’s looping kinds (categories that change their referents by being applied)
